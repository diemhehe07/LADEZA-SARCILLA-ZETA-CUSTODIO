Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about SLS-U Matter, counseling, and how to use this platform as an SLSU Lucena student.

If you don’t see your question here, you can always reach out through the Contact page or speak directly with the Guidance and Counseling Office.

Who is SLS-U Matter for?

SLS-U Matter is designed primarily for students of Southern Luzon State University (SLSU) Lucena campus. Some information may also be helpful to faculty, staff, or parents who want to understand available support.

Is this website a replacement for counseling or therapy?

No. SLS-U Matter offers tools, information, and a way to connect with campus support, but it does not replace one-on-one counseling, medical care, or professional diagnosis. It’s a starting point to help you reach the right kind of support.

How do I book a counseling or guidance session?

You can use the Booking page to request a session. Choose the type of support you need, select a time that fits your schedule, and provide your basic details. A staff member may contact you to confirm or adjust the schedule depending on availability.

Are my details and concerns kept confidential?

Information you share is handled with care and is generally treated as confidential, especially for counseling-related concerns. However, there may be exceptions if there is a serious risk of harm to yourself or others, or when required by law or institutional policy. For more information, see the Privacy Policy.

What if I need urgent or emergency help?

If you are in immediate danger or feel you might harm yourself or someone else, please contact local emergency services or crisis hotlines right away. You may also go directly to the SLSU Guidance and Counseling Office during office hours.

Can my parents or guardians see my information?

This depends on your age, local laws, and university policies. In many cases, your counseling information is treated as confidential, but there may be situations where involving a parent, guardian, or another trusted adult is important for your safety and support.

Do I have to use my real name and student information?

For formal bookings or follow-ups, real details are usually needed so staff can contact you and verify your student status. For general browsing or some feedback features, you may be able to remain anonymous.

Does using SLS-U Matter cost anything?

Access to the website and its core functions is free for students. Counseling and guidance services are typically provided as part of the university’s student support programs. If any costs apply for specific services, they will be communicated by the university.

Can I access SLS-U Matter on my phone?

Yes. The site is designed to be mobile-friendly so you can access resources and booking features from your phone, tablet, or computer.

How can I give feedback or suggest improvements?

You can use the Feedback page to share your experience, suggest new features, or report technical issues. Your feedback helps us make SLS-U Matter more useful for students.

The information in these FAQs is general and may not cover all situations. For specific concerns, especially involving safety, health, or legal matters, please speak directly with a qualified professional or the appropriate university office.
