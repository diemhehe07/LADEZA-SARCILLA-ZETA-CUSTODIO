Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how SLS-U Matter collects, uses, and protects information when you use our website and related services.

Last updated: March 2025 • This is an informational policy and does not replace official university policies.

1. Who we are

SLS-U Matter is a student-focused mental health support platform created for Southern Luzon State University (SLSU) Lucena students. The site aims to provide information, resources, and a way to connect with campus-based support services such as the Guidance and Counseling Office.

This website is designed for informational and support purposes only and is not a substitute for professional diagnosis, treatment, or emergency services.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide directly

When you use SLS-U Matter, you may provide information such as:

  • Basic contact details (name, email address, phone number)
  • Student-related information (course, year level, student ID – if requested)
  • Details submitted through booking forms, feedback forms, or contact forms
  • Voluntary information about your concerns, preferences, or experiences

2.2 Automatically collected information

Depending on how the site is configured, we may collect:

  • Technical information such as browser type, device type, and approximate location
  • Usage data, such as pages visited, time spent on the site, and interactions with resources

2.3 Sensitive information

Some information you share (for example in booking, feedback, or chat) may relate to your mental health or personal experiences. We treat this as sensitive and handle it with care, but please avoid sharing details that identify other people without their consent.

3. How we use your information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Schedule and manage counseling or guidance-related appointments
  • Respond to your messages and feedback
  • Improve the quality and relevance of services and resources
  • Generate anonymized statistics about usage and student needs
  • Maintain the security and reliability of the website

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

4. Data storage and security

SLS-U Matter may use third-party services such as Firebase to store data securely. These providers implement technical safeguards such as encryption and access controls. However, no online system can be guaranteed 100% secure.

Access to identifiable information is limited to authorized staff or personnel who need it to support students or maintain the system.

5. Cookies and analytics

If analytics tools or cookies are used, they are primarily for understanding how the site is used (for example, which pages are most visited) so we can improve content and services. You can usually control cookies through your browser settings.

6. Data retention

We retain data only for as long as it is reasonably needed for the purposes described in this policy, for internal reporting, or to comply with applicable institutional or legal requirements. Where possible, information is anonymized or aggregated.

7. Your choices and rights

Subject to applicable laws and university policies, you may:

  • Request to update or correct your personal details
  • Request more information about how your data is used
  • Ask for certain data (such as feedback) to be removed, where appropriate

To make a request, please contact the SLSU Guidance and Counseling Office or use the Contact page on this website. Some requests may need to be handled through official university channels.

8. Third-party links and services

SLS-U Matter may link to external websites or services (for example, crisis hotlines, mental health resources, or university portals). We are not responsible for the privacy practices, security, or content of these third-party sites.

9. Use by minors

This site is intended for SLSU students and may be accessed by individuals who are 18 or older, or younger students as allowed by university policies. If you are under 18, we encourage you to involve a trusted adult when seeking support, especially for serious or emergency concerns.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in services, technology, or legal requirements. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page will be revised when changes are made.

11. Contact

For questions about this Privacy Policy or how your information is used, you may contact the SLSU Guidance and Counseling Office or send a message through the Contact page of SLS-U Matter. Please avoid including very sensitive or emergency information in written messages.
